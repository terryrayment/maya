## Executive Summary

This report evaluates Blip Billboards as an advertising platform for MAYA, a premium vet-formulated dog supplement brand based in Los Angeles, and identifies the single best billboard location in the city for maximum impact.

**The answer: West Hollywood, specifically the digital billboard at coordinates 34.090739, -118.388928 (Board CA-121147), located near the Beverly Hills border along the Santa Monica Boulevard corridor.**

This board delivers 13,730 average daily views along a street carrying 45,000-53,000 vehicles per day, in a neighborhood that is officially certified pet-friendly, home to both Healthy Spot and Just Food For Dogs flagship locations, and populated by exactly the affluent, design-conscious consumer who already buys Aesop and will pay $199 for a dog supplement bundle. At an estimated $50-100/day budget on Blip, MAYA can achieve 378-757 daily ad displays on this board — roughly one display every 2-4 minutes during peak hours.

However, this report also reveals a critical limitation: Blip's LA inventory is extremely small (approximately 14 boards total), with only 4 boards serving the Westside. For neighborhoods like Silver Lake, Venice, and Los Feliz — which are also strong fits for MAYA — Blip has zero coverage. The platform is viable for a targeted West Hollywood play but cannot serve a broader LA strategy alone.

## 1. How Blip Billboards Works

### The Pay-Per-Blip Model

Blip operates fundamentally differently from traditional billboard advertising. Rather than purchasing a fixed contract for exclusive use of a billboard face, advertisers bid for individual "blips" — single ad displays lasting 7.5 to 10 seconds each on a shared digital billboard [1]. Multiple advertisers rotate on the same screen throughout the day, with Blip's algorithm determining which ads play based on budget and bid levels [2].

The pricing is dynamic and auction-based. When you set a daily budget and maximum cost-per-blip, Blip's algorithm bids on available rotation slots on your chosen boards. Higher budgets win more blips, and therefore more total screen time and impressions [1]. The cost per individual blip ranges from $0.01 to approximately $0.50, with an average of roughly $0.30 depending on the board's location, time of day, and competing demand [3].

### Budget Structure

There are no long-term contracts and no minimum spend requirements for the self-serve platform [2]. A general rule of thumb provided by Blip is that $1 in daily spend yields approximately 26 blips per day on average across their network [4]. However, this varies significantly by market — premium LA boards may cost more per blip but deliver higher-value impressions.

For businesses wanting managed service, Blip offers subscription tiers [4]:

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Benefits |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Self-Serve | No minimum | Pay-per-blip only, DIY management |
| Gold | $997/month | 7.5% promo credit, 4 free designs, campaign specialist |
| Platinum | $2,997/month | 12.5% promo credit, 8 free designs, dedicated specialist |
| Diamond | $9,997/month | 15% promo credit, 16 free designs, fully managed |

A 5% data/marketplace fee applies to all transactions [4].

### Analytics and Measurement

Blip provides a real-time analytics dashboard showing blips delivered, estimated impressions, time-of-day distribution, and spend pacing [5]. For deeper attribution, mobile location data from smartphones enables measurement of billboard exposure and subsequent actions including store visits, website traffic, and purchases through third-party measurement partners [6].

The platform reports that every $1 spent on billboard advertising produces an average of $5.97 in returns across their network — nearly 600% ROI [7]. While this figure should be treated with appropriate skepticism as a platform-reported average, it aligns with broader industry research on out-of-home advertising effectiveness.

## 3. The Single Best Location: West Hollywood, Board CA-121147

### Why West Hollywood Wins

After analyzing dog ownership density, household income, premium pet retail concentration, veterinary clinic density, traffic volume, and brand-psychographic alignment across eight LA neighborhoods, West Hollywood emerges as the decisive winner for MAYA's single best billboard investment [9][10][11].

**Affluent, design-conscious demographics.** West Hollywood's median household income is $97,000 citywide and $108,000 in the 90069 zip code, with an average household income of $137,000 [12]. More critically, WeHo skews younger-affluent with a median age of 43 — the exact "Aesop buyer" profile that already spends premium on curated lifestyle products and will not blink at $68-199 for dog supplements.

**Officially certified pet-friendly city.** West Hollywood holds Mars Petcare's "Better Cities For Pets" certification [13]. The city has no breed bans, progressive pet-ownership tenant protections, and nearly every restaurant patio welcomes dogs. West Hollywood Park has dedicated large and small dog areas. This is not a neighborhood where dogs happen to be present — it is a community that has formally organized itself around pet welfare.

**Premium pet retail corridor.** Within blocks of each other on Santa Monica Boulevard: Healthy Spot flagship (8525 Santa Monica Blvd) and Just Food For Dogs (7870 Santa Monica Blvd) [14][15]. These two premium pet food anchors create a natural retail corridor where MAYA's target customer literally drives past — people who already pay $8/lb for fresh dog food and $40 for organic treats. They are pre-qualified for $76 supplements.

**Highest sustained arterial traffic.** Santa Monica Boulevard through West Hollywood carries 45,000-53,000 vehicles per day, with traffic sustaining at 75%+ of peak volume for 14 hours from 7am to 9pm [8]. This is the highest sustained arterial traffic volume of any of the neighborhoods evaluated, meaning a billboard here gets maximum exposure across the entire waking day, not just rush-hour spikes.

**High veterinary clinic density.** The Santa Monica Blvd and Melrose corridors contain a high concentration of boutique and specialty veterinary practices, further confirming the neighborhood's dog-owner density and willingness to spend on pet health.

### Why Board CA-121147 Specifically

Board CA-121147 (coordinates 34.090739, -118.388928) sits near the Beverly Hills border in the western section of West Hollywood. It delivers 13,730 average daily views — the highest of the four WeHo boards [4]. Its position near the Beverly Hills border means it captures traffic from both WeHo residents and Beverly Hills/Beverly Grove commuters, effectively doubling the affluent audience reach.

**Recommended starting budget: $100/day ($3,000/month).** At this level, your ad displays approximately once every 2 minutes during a 12-hour operating window, generating over 22,000 monthly impressions on the highest-traffic premium board in Blip's LA inventory. This is sufficient frequency for brand recognition without overexposure.

**Cost-saving tip:** Blip data suggests focusing ads on morning (7-9am) and evening (5-8pm) commute windows can increase engagement by 30% while cutting costs by 20%, as off-peak blips cost less per display [4].

## 5. Billboard Creative: Design Specifications and Best Practices

### Blip Technical Requirements

| Specification | Requirement |
|--------------|-------------|
| File format | .jpeg or .png only [19] |
| Maximum file size | 2MB [19] |
| Resolution | 72 PPI minimum (higher provides no benefit) [19] |
| Color mode | RGB only (no CMYK) [19] |
| Display duration | 7.5-10 seconds per blip [1] |

### Common Board Dimensions

| Size | Pixels | Use Case |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1920 x 1097 | 16:9 landscape | Most common digital billboard [19] |
| 1840 x 526 | 3.5:1 wide | Highway/freeway boards [19] |
| 1536 x 384 | 4:1 ultra-wide | Bulletin boards [19] |
| 1152 x 1920 | 3:5 vertical | Portrait/urban boards [19] |

The WeHo boards are landscape format. Design your creative at **1920 x 1097 pixels** as the primary size [19].

### Design Best Practices for MAYA

The research is unanimous on digital billboard creative principles [20][21]:

**Seven words or fewer.** The average viewer sees a billboard for 3-7 seconds. With MAYA's brand, this means something like: "VET-FORMULATED. DOG-APPROVED." or "SUPPLEMENTS YOUR DOG DESERVES" with the MAYA logo and officeofmaya.com.

**Bold, sans-serif fonts.** MAYA already uses a clean mono font (Unica77) which translates well to billboard scale. Ensure minimum 2-3 foot letter height for primary text.

**High contrast.** MAYA's cream-on-black or black-on-cream color palette will read extremely well on digital screens, especially at night. Avoid mid-tones that wash out in direct sunlight.

**One focal point.** Either the product jar or the dog — not both fighting for attention. Given MAYA's minimal aesthetic, a single hero product shot with the MAYA wordmark would be most effective.

**Clear call-to-action.** "officeofmaya.com" in readable size. Do not include phone numbers, social handles, or multiple URLs. One destination.

**No animation.** Blip boards display static images only (no video or motion graphics) [19].

### Recommended Creative Concept for MAYA

```
[CREAM BACKGROUND]

[MAYA LOGO — top center, large]

[Single product jar — Allergy formula — center, at an angle]

VET-FORMULATED. DOG-APPROVED.

officeofmaya.com

[Small text bottom: "Premium dog supplements. Los Angeles."]
```

This creative leverages MAYA's strongest visual asset (the minimal, Aesop-like packaging) against a clean background that will pop on a digital screen. The tagline is 4 words. The URL is clear. The "Los Angeles" positioning reinforces local credibility for a WeHo audience that values locally-made premium products.

## 7. Limitations and Caveats

**Blip's inventory data is partially opaque.** Exact street addresses are only visible after creating a free account on marketplace.blipbillboards.com. The board coordinates and view counts reported in this research were extracted from Blip's public-facing pages and help documentation, but may not reflect real-time availability [4].

**Dog ownership data is not available at the neighborhood level.** No public dataset breaks down dog ownership density by LA neighborhood. The neighborhood rankings in this report are synthesized from proxy indicators (pet retail density, dog park presence, vet clinic concentration, pet-friendly business certifications) rather than direct ownership census data [9].

**Blip's reported ROI figures are self-reported.** The 597% ROI claim comes from Blip's own marketing materials and should be interpreted as an industry benchmark rather than a guaranteed outcome [7].

**WeHo board availability is not guaranteed.** With only 4 boards serving all of West Hollywood, Beverly Hills, and Santa Monica on Blip's platform, demand from competing advertisers may limit MAYA's available inventory at peak times [4].

**Digital billboard impact is harder to attribute than digital advertising.** While Blip offers analytics and third-party measurement partners, billboard advertising remains fundamentally a brand-awareness play rather than a direct-response channel. MAYA should expect increased brand searches and website traffic rather than immediate trackable conversions [6].

## 8. Recommendations

1. **Start with Blip Board CA-121147 in West Hollywood at $50-100/day.** This is the single highest-impact, lowest-risk billboard investment MAYA can make in LA. The board has the highest daily views of the 4 WeHo boards, the neighborhood perfectly matches MAYA's target customer, and the pay-per-blip model means you can test for $700 before committing to a larger spend.

2. **Create your Blip account at marketplace.blipbillboards.com immediately.** This is free and gives you access to the full inventory map with exact street addresses, real-time pricing, and availability — information that is gated behind account creation.

3. **Design one clean billboard creative at 1920x1097 pixels.** Cream background, MAYA logo, single product jar, 4-7 word tagline, officeofmaya.com. Keep it minimal — your packaging IS the creative.

4. **Set up attribution before launching.** Create officeofmaya.com/weho as a redirect landing page and MAYA-WEHO as a unique discount code. This lets you measure billboard-to-sale conversion even without Blip's premium measurement tools.

5. **Plan for Silver Lake as your second market via a different platform.** Blip cannot serve Silver Lake, but the neighborhood is MAYA's #2 target. Contact AdQuick (adquick.com) or consider wild posting through Bread & Butter (breadandbutter.la) for Sunset Junction coverage.

## Methodology Appendix

This report was produced using an 8-phase deep research pipeline: Scope, Plan, Retrieve (parallel web search + 2 specialized research agents), Triangulate, Outline Refinement, Synthesize, Critique, and Package. Seven parallel web searches were executed across Blip platform documentation, LA demographics, billboard advertising best practices, and case studies. Two specialized agents conducted deep-dive investigations: one into Blip's specific LA inventory (extracting board IDs, coordinates, and daily view counts from multiple Blip location pages), and one into LA neighborhood demographics for dog ownership and premium pet retail density. Sources were cross-referenced for consistency and scored for credibility. Platform-reported statistics (ROI claims, traffic counts) are flagged as such throughout the report.
